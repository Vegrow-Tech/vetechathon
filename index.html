FAQs_

What is the recommended team size for the hackathon?

Teams should ideally consist of 3-5 members, with a minimum of 3 and a maximum of 5 participants. Every member listed in the team should actively participate in the hackathon, and no leaves during the event will be approved.

Is it mandatory to have a team name?

Yes, each team should have a unique name at the time of registration.

How can teams access the code base during the hackathon?

Teams will be provided access to version control systems (e.g., Git) and project management platforms to facilitate collaboration and code sharing among team members.

Is documentation encouraged for the projects?

Yes, teams are encouraged to document their project ideas, progress and decisions using collaborative tools such as Google Docs, Trello, or Slack channels.

Will mentorship be available during the hackathon?

Mentorship will be available to assist teams throughout the hackathon.

How much does it cost?

Everything at VeTechathon is free of cost.

What are the expectations for the Minimum Viable Product (MVP)?

Teams should aim to create a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) during the hackathon. The MVP should demonstrate core functionalities, features, or algorithms that address the problem statement and provide value to users.

How should teams demonstrate their prototypes ?

At the end of the hackathon, all teams should be prepared to present their prototypes or pitches to the judges. Teams should effectively demonstrate their project's functionality, user interface, and value proposition during the presentation.

Will teams receive feedback on their projects?

Yes, Teams should be open to receiving feedback from judges and other participants. Teams should use the feedback received to iterate and improve their project concept or prototype.

How should teams align with the judging criteria?

Teams should align their project goals and presentations with the hackathon's judging criteria, focusing on innovation, impact, feasibility, user experience, technical excellence, functionality, presentation quality, collaboration, adaptability, and overall value proposition.

Is it mandatory for the support team to join any hackathon team?

No, the support team is not required to join any hackathon team. They can serve as a knowledge source about our existing problems and our current system. While they are exclusive to building a prototype that requires technical knowledge, they have the option to join any team if they wish.

Can the QA team form their own team for the hackathon?

Yes, the QA team has the option to form their own team if they want to innovate within their domain.

Can the QA team collaborate with other teams?

Absolutely, the QA team is encouraged to collaborate with other teams to contribute their expertise and support in achieving project goals.
